A Letter from Steve Blakeman

Volunteer Team Leader

I, Steve Blakeman, and Ty are putting together a "lite" construction Team for a short-term project (related to COTA). Please read the story below and see if the Lord speaks to you about this opportunity to be a huge blessing to a relatively new Believer at COTA. Trust me, if you can join us, YOU will walk away having been blessed in a major way.

Here's the story:

I've known Ty, who has been attending Apostles, for about 3 years. I've had the privilege of watching him grow spiritually by leaps and bounds over the past 2½ years.

About 3 years ago Ty's house sustained considerable damage due to a fire. He used the insurance money to make most of the repairs himself. Even though it's been 3 years, Ty still has some final changes that need to be made. He, being a single father of a 4-year-old, and having started a new construction business a couple of years ago, his spare time to do the finishing touches is minimal.

It was suggested by Kevin Miller, a friend of Ty's from Apostles, that it would be a major help to Ty to help him finish the undone repairs around his house. (We can all relate to what it's like to live in a house that "needs work," while not having adequate spare time to get to those pesky chores.) So, when I suggested to Ty what Kevin proposed, he was amazed at such an offer.

Ty and I have chosen the weekend of April 25-26 to get a band of people together to lend a helping hand. His son, Oliver, is staying with Ty's parents this entire month (in High Point) so childcare is not an issue. Between Ty and I we will have the tools needed to complete the projects.

Now, this is where you come in:

Ty and I hope to create a team of about 10 people to join this effort. It will not only include such things as swinging a hammer and using a paint brush, but also providing 3 meals so the "hands on" workers won't have to focus on food prep.

We don't expect any of you to commit to both days, or even to the entirety of either day. Just come when you can and stay as long as fits your schedule. Joining us for even a couple of hours would be great.

I'm hoping that you will see where you can fit into this Team.

Steve Blakeman

Volunteer Team Leader

Project Details

Here are the projects we'll be tackling. Find one that matches your skills or try something new with guidance from the team!

Installing Baseboards

Scope of Work

  • Measure all wall lengths and cut baseboards to fit
  • Cope or miter inside/outside corners
  • Nail baseboards to wall studs
  • +3 more steps...
Installing Door Trim (Casing)

Scope of Work

  • Measure and cut casing for each door
  • Maintain consistent reveal (~3/16") from door jamb
  • Miter corners at 45°
  • +2 more steps...
Caulk and Paint Trim

Scope of Work

  • Caulk all seams: trim to wall, inside corners
  • Smooth caulk lines
  • Spot prime if needed
  • +1 more steps...
Route Door Latch & Strike Plate

Scope of Work

  • Mark latch plate outline on door
  • Use router or chisel to recess latch plate flush
  • Repeat for strike plate on door jamb
  • +1 more steps...
Replace Laundry Room Flooring (30 sqft)

Scope of Work

  • Remove existing flooring
  • Inspect and prep subfloor
  • Install new flooring (LVP or similar)
  • +2 more steps...
Paint Exterior of Shed

Scope of Work

  • Clean surface (remove dirt/debris)
  • Scrape loose paint
  • Prime bare wood if needed
  • +1 more steps...
Hang Can Light

Scope of Work

  • Cut hole in ceiling
  • Run wiring from existing power source
  • Install can light housing
  • +2 more steps...
Relocate Light Fixture (Attic Access)

Scope of Work

  • Turn off power
  • Remove existing fixture
  • Access attic and install new junction box
  • +4 more steps...
Patch Drywall

Scope of Work

  • Cut clean edges around damaged areas
  • Install backing if needed
  • Screw in drywall patch
  • +4 more steps...

Tools will be provided by Ty and Steve

Just bring yourself and a willing heart to serve!
